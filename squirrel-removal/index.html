Our Professional Squirrel Removal Process

Our approach to effective squirrel removal in Baldwin Park is thorough and systematic. We follow a proven multi-step process to ensure all squirrels are safely removed and steps are taken to prevent their return.

Comprehensive Inspection and Assessment

The first critical step is a detailed inspection of your property. Our technician will carefully examine your home or business to identify signs of squirrel activity, including:

Identifying Entry Points

Squirrels can squeeze into surprisingly small openings. We'll meticulously search for entry points around your roofline, eaves, vents, chimneys, and any other potential access points that squirrels are utilizing. This crucial step helps us understand how they are getting in and out.

Assessing the Extent of Infestation

During the assessment, we'll determine the severity of the squirrel problem, including the number of squirrels present, the location of nests, and the level of damage already inflicted.

Evaluating Damaged Areas

Squirrels can cause significant damage by gnawing on wood, insulation, and electrical wiring. We will assess all damaged areas to provide a complete picture of the impact of the infestation. This assessment is vital for planning the most effective removal and repair strategy.

Humane Squirrel Trapping

Based on our assessment, we will implement a humane trapping strategy. We utilize live traps placed strategically in areas of high squirrel activity. Our goal is to capture the squirrels without causing harm.

Strategic Trap Placement

Traps are placed in areas where squirrels are known to travel, enter, or exit your property. This could include attics, rooftops, or near damaged areas where they are gaining access. Proper placement is key to successful and efficient trapping.

Regular Trap Monitoring

We understand the importance of minimizing the time squirrels spend in traps. Our technicians regularly monitor the traps, typically within 24 hours of placement, to ensure timely removal and relocation of captured squirrels.

Safe and Humane Relocation

Once squirrels are captured, we prioritize their safe and humane relocation to a suitable natural habitat away from your property. We adhere to all local regulations regarding wildlife relocation.

Transporting Captured Squirrels

Captured squirrels are transported in a secure and stress-free manner to a location several miles away from your Baldwin Park property to prevent their return.

Choosing Appropriate Relocation Sites

We select relocation sites that provide the squirrels with the necessary resources for survival and are situated in areas where they will not become a nuisance to others.

Habitat Modification and Exclusion

Removing the squirrels is only half the battle. Preventing their return is equally important. Our team will identify and seal all entry points to make your property squirrel-proof.

Repairing Entry Points

We'll repair holes, gaps, and damaged areas that squirrels used to enter your property. This may involve using durable materials that squirrels cannot easily chew through.

Installing Exclusion Devices

In some cases, we may install exclusion devices that allow squirrels to exit but prevent them from re-entering your property. This is a particularly effective method for ensuring all squirrels have left before permanently sealing access points.

Providing Preventative Recommendations

Our technicians will offer expert advice on how to make your property less attractive to squirrels in the future, such as trimming tree branches that overhang your roof or securing garbage cans.

Addressing a squirrel infestation promptly is crucial to prevent further damage and potential health risks. Our detailed process for squirrel removal in Baldwin Park, CA is designed to provide a lasting solution.

One of the most common indicators of squirrels on your property is the presence of strange noises. Chewed Wood and Wiring

Squirrels are rodents, and like all rodents, they possess a strong instinct to chew. This behavior is not just destructive; it's essential for them to keep their continuously growing incisors at a manageable length. When squirrels infest a property, they often chew on various materials, including wood structures, insulation, and alarmingly, electrical wiring. Damages to wood trim, siding, or structural beams can compromise the integrity of your building. Chewing on electrical wires is particularly dangerous, as it poses a significant fire hazard. Exposed wires can spark, leading to short circuits or even igniting flammable materials in your attic or walls. The risk associated with damaged wiring is extremely high and requires immediate attention from both pest control professionals and potentially electricians. Identifying areas where wood or wires have been gnawed is a clear indication of squirrel presence and active infestation. Look for wood shavings, exposed wires, or frayed insulation.
